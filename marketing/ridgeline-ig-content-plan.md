# RidgeLine — Instagram Launch Content Plan

**Version 1 — for beta launch phase**

---

## Positioning

**One-line pitch:** RidgeLine is MTB-specific training that actually gets you faster — strength, mobility, skills, and trail intelligence in one app.

**Who it's for:**
- Weekend warriors who want to be measurably faster on their local trails
- Racers (XC, enduro, DH) who need real periodization, not generic endurance plans
- Mid-life athletes fighting mobility loss and prehab-ing chronic injuries
- Skills-focused riders who know off-bike work matters but don't know what to do

**What we're NOT:** Strava (that's a log, not a coach). TrainingPeaks (built for road/tri, MTB is an afterthought). YouTube tutorials (unstructured, no progression).

**The angle to lean on:** *We're the only training system built specifically for how mountain bikers actually get better.*

---

## Content Pillars

Rotate through these four — never let one dominate. Aim for roughly balanced weekly output.

### 1. The Product (30% of posts)
Screenshots, feature walkthroughs, before/after data views. This is where the glassy UI does the heavy lifting.

### 2. The Craft (30% of posts)
MTB-specific training education. Why single-leg strength matters for descending. How mobility loss steals your race pace. The kettlebell swing → climb-power connection. This is where you build authority — you're not selling, you're teaching.

### 3. The Journey (25% of posts)
Building in public. Roadmap moves, feature releases, "here's what I shipped this week," honest struggles ("Garmin still hasn't approved us"). This is where personality lives. Solo founder + real story is your unfair advantage.

### 4. The Trails (15% of posts)
Actual ride footage, POV, trail profile screenshots, local trail nerdery. Shows you're a rider first, not just a maker. Grounds the brand.

---

## Cadence

Sustainable schedule for a solo founder:

| Day | Content type |
|-----|--------------|
| **Mon** | Feed post — usually Craft (education kicks off the week) |
| **Tue** | Reel — Product feature demo |
| **Wed** | Feed post — Product or Trails |
| **Thu** | Story series — behind the scenes |
| **Fri** | Feed post — Journey (weekly progress) |
| **Sat** | Reel — Craft (a specific exercise, drill, or tip) |
| **Sun** | Carousel — weekly recap of your training + rides through RidgeLine's lens |

**Stories every day** — quick polls, behind the scenes, "what am I working on today", coffee + laptop shots. Low effort, keeps you top of feed.

## Hashtag Strategy

Instagram counts hashtags in the caption OR first comment. Use **10-15 per post**, rotate three sets so you don't look automated. Mix big/medium/small.

**Set A — Discovery (big volume, use sparingly):**
`#mountainbiking #mtb #mtblife #mountainbiker #mtbtraining`

**Set B — Niche (medium, use often):**
`#enduromtb #xcracing #mtbskills #mtbcoach #bikefitness #mtbtechnique #trailrider #mtbstrength`

**Set C — Community (small, use always):**
`#ridgelinemtb #mtbtrainingapp #getfasteronthebike #dirtjumper #mtbperformance #mountainbikeconditioning`

**Rotate:** Set A + C on Monday/Wednesday/Friday feed posts. Set B + C on Reels. Set C only on Stories. Add 2-3 hyper-local tags for your area: `#squamishmtb`, `#whistlermtb`, etc.

## Metrics to Watch

Don't chase followers. Watch these instead:

- **Profile visits** — are people curious enough to check you out?
- **Link-in-bio clicks** — that's the funnel to your app
- **Beta sign-ups tagged from IG** — the number that actually matters
- **DMs asking questions** — the highest-intent signal

Post more of whatever drives DMs and beta sign-ups. Post less of whatever gets likes but doesn't convert. Vanity metrics lie.
